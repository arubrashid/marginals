# clusion

>*(noun; plural, unbounded)*

A form of writing that gestures toward conclusion without enacting [linear] finality, much like its own word form—it is liminal, and in such liminality there is much comfort. 

Clusions may gowned as essays, papers, or declarations, but remain porous, recursive, and metabolically unfinished. 

Though they may echo authority, they often fray or subvert it, destabilising their own apparent closure.

The term emerges from conclusion stripped of closure, tangled with inclusion, occlusion, and side-along traces of seclusion and preclusion.
