# of no good fortune

There **was** a door.    

A door that **was**, , **is**, glorious;    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;wooden feathers and kilted claws, cradled hums and soothing lulls.    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A balm of glory, hinged precariously on a simple instruction:    

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;*the door must remain closed*.   

As with all instructions,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;they are,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;**of no good fortune**,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;taken as mere suggestions.   

One day, the precarious hinges were dusted and turned.   
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The door’s kilted claws creaked with pressure,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;its feathers splintered, and the lulls ceased    
—a cradled hollowing.    

But it did not resist, for it **was**, , **is**, simply a door.   

And so, the door opened with fault,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and it did not take a moment,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;nor a second, before it wailed.    

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The door wailed in dew and disparage.    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It wailed for what was and what is.    

**For**, **it was**, **no longer a door**, ,, **it is**, **no longer anything**.
