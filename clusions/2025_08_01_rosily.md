>##
>
>### **ONTICITY** 
>>
>>**WHEREBY**: 
>>>a rose is ontic. 
>>
>>**WHEREIN**: 
>>>“that which is”, is ontic. 
>>
>>**BY, FOURTRAIN**:
>>>a rose is there, in the world. 
>>>it exists materially. 
>>>it can be held. 
>>>it can be cut. 
>>>it can be smelt.
>>>it is form. 
>>>it is matter. 
>>>it is phenotype.
>>
>>**IN SO**: 
>>>onticity is the material fact. 
>>>the petals, the thorns, the scent: ontic facts.
>>>this is the rose-as-thing: the noun prior to description.
>
>##
>
>### **ONTOLOGY** 
>>
>>**WHEREBY**: 
>>>roseness is ontological.
>>
>>**WHEREIN**: 
>>>“that which it means to be”, is ontological. 
>>
>>**BY, FOURTRAIN**:
>>>roseness is being-a-rose.
>>>not just the scent, but: 
>>>
>>>>the affect, 
>>>>the associations, 
>>>>the poetic valence, 
>>>>the symbolic inheritance, 
>>>>the metaphysical weight of the rose.
>>
>>**IN SO**: 
>>>ontology is the structure and condition of its being. 
>>>it is the romanticism, 
>>>
>>>>the beauty, 
>>>>the fragility, 
>>>>the seduction, 
>>>>the death.
>>>
>>>it is not just the rose, 
>>>>but what the rose becomes when it is thought of.
>>>
>>>it is what the rose means 
>>>>in culture, 
>>>>in literature, 
>>>>in theology, 
>>>>in memory.
>>>
>>>ontological thinking asks: 
>>>>what is it to be a rose? 
>>>>what conditions make a rose “a rose”,
>>>>>rather than a weed, or a lily, or a name? 
>>>>>or, perhaps it is a name. 
>>>>>>—perhaps it is your name. 
>
>##
>
>### **AFACTICE**
>>
>>**WHEREBY**: 
>>>a way of seeing a rose by what is seen to be a rose, 
>>>but a rose was refused the right to name itself;
>>>thereby, is it actually a rose? 
>>	
>>**WHEREIN**: 
>>>“that which we call a rose…”, is afactice on ontic. 
>>
>>**BY, FOURTRAIN**:
>>>a rose must be called a rose in order to be acknowledged: 
>>>
>>>>the name is necessary, because: 
>>>>>how else could we even talk about it?
>>>>>how else would it be fragranced? 
>>>>>how else would it be vased in a kitchen? 
>>>>
>>>>but, what happens when we call it a rose;
>>>>
>>>>>what is lost?
>>>>>what is forced?
>>>>>what becomes unavailable?
>>
>>**IN SO**: 
>>>afactice is the metabolic weight of being named. 
>>>it is a way of seeing what has already been seen, 
>>>>but refused the right to name itself.
>>>
>>>it doesn’t just question: what the rose is. 
>>>it asks what has been assigned: 
>>>
>>>>is the assignment premature?
>>>>has the assignment been metabolised as identity? 
>>>>is the assigned identify by fact?  
>>>>yet, it holds the assigned: 
>>>>
>>>>>by way of name, 
>>>>>by way of tag, 
>>>>>by way of expectation, and 
>>>>>by way of projection into form.
>>>
>>>but, it is not in opposition to a name, 
>>>>it simply refuses to let naming be mistaken for essence.
>>>
>>>afactice says: 
>>>>you needed the label to touch roseness, 
>>>>but in doing so, you also distorted the thing you meant to love.
>>

>>
>>>A love not unlike that of a rose.

>>>in desire,

>>>It is a story told by desire, non-local and entangled.

>>What a tragedy indeed—
>>>for, a tragedy earthed, 
>>>and ne’er lips touched by poison.
>>>for, Juliet never asked to be rose-sworn.

>>>>*O Romeo, Romeo, wherefore art thou* Romeo?

>>>for what is a rose?

>>To which one may decide: 
>>
>>>It is what Romeo needed it to be. 
>>>It is what neither Rosaline nor Juliet were allowed to define. 
>>>It is what Romeo too stands victim of.
>>
>>And, is this not the very nature of afactice?
