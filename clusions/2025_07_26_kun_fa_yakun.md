# kūn fa-yakūn

‘[Who Has Seen the Wind?](./2025_07_26_cerebral.md)’ by Christina Rossetti, **I**,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;**and perhaps you too**,    
much do admire.    

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;—for is not the body an arrangement by water,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;and if words are oil,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;then such a poem must float,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;cerebral in mind.    

And, **I**,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;**and perhaps you too**,    
do wonder: is it only once the mind is free from oil, can the wind be seen?    

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Where, seen is not seen, but to simply, just, be.    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And, be is `kūn fa-yakūn`—but as return.      

Though, in such return there is much sorrow, for words are all **I**,    
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;**and perhaps you too**,    
have ever known.
